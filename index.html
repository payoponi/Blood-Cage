History of the UFC

The UFC started back in 1993 as a wild, no-rules fight competition to see which martial art was the best. It was raw, brutal, and a bit of a mess. People called it "human cockfighting," and it almost got shut down everywhere. They had no money, no fans, and most states banned it. In 2001, Dana White and the Fertitta brothers bought the UFC for $2 million, which was basically nothing. They cleaned it up, made rules, and worked hard to get the sport legit. The big break came in 2005 with The Ultimate Fighter show. A crazy fight between Griffin and Bonnar, It blew up and put the UFC on the map. After that, they kept growing, buying out their competition like Pride and Strikeforce, and signing some of the best fighters in the world. By 2016, the UFC was sold for $4 billion. Now, it’s one of the biggest sports companies, with stars like Chael Sonnen and Jon Jones pulling in millions of fans. From almost nothing to dominating the game.

History of the mma

MMA’s been around forever if you think about it. Back in ancient Greece, they had pankration, which was like MMA before it had a name. Later, in the 1900s, Brazil gave us "Vale Tudo" no rules fighting where the Gracies showed off Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. In Japan, they had their own version with shoot wrestling. Things got real in 1993 when the UFC showed up. It was a wild tournament to see which fighting style was king. Over time, fighters stopped sticking to one style and started mixing it up, striking, grappling, submissions, everything. By the 2000s, MMA blew up. Pride was huge in Japan, and the UFC became a monster after getting organized. Now, MMA’s one of the biggest sports in the world, with fighters who can do it all. It’s come a long way from the old days of no rules and no money.

Top 10 main events

UFC 100

Lesnar vs Mir 2

At UFC 100 in 2009, Brock Lesnar rematched Frank Mir after losing to him in their first fight. Lesnar dominated the fight, controlling Mir on the ground and finishing him with a TKO in the second round. It was a dominant performance that secured his spot as UFC Heavyweight Champion.

UFC 129

St-Pierre vs Shields

At UFC 129 in 2011, Georges St-Pierre easily defended his welterweight title against Jake Shields, dominating the fight with his striking and wrestling. St-Pierre won by unanimous decision, solidifying his legacy as one of the best in the sport.

UFC nigth

McGregor vs Brandao

At UFC Fight Night in 2014, Conor McGregor faced Diego Brandao in his first main event. McGregor put on a show, finishing Brandao in the first round with a TKO. It was a statement win that launched McGregor's rise to stardom in the UFC.

UFC 189

McGregor vs Mendes

At UFC 189 in 2015, Conor McGregor faced Chad Mendes for the interim featherweight title. McGregor overcame early adversity, knocking Mendes out in the second round to claim the belt. It was a thrilling fight that showcased McGregor's striking and heart.

UFC 194

McGregor vs Aldo

At UFC 194 in 2015, Conor McGregor faced Jose Aldo for the featherweight title. McGregor shocked the world, knocking Aldo out in just 13 seconds to become the undisputed champion. It was a historic moment that solidified McGregor as a superstar in the sport.

UFC nigth

Dos Anjos vs Cerrone 2

At UFC Fight Night in 2015, Rafael dos Anjos defended his lightweight title against Donald Cerrone in a rematch. Dos Anjos dominated the fight, finishing Cerrone in the first round with strikes. It was a dominant performance that showcased Dos Anjos' skills as a champion.

UFC 205

McGregor vs Alvarez

At UFC 205 in 2016, Conor McGregor faced Eddie Alvarez for the lightweight title. McGregor made history, becoming the first fighter to hold two belts in two different weight classes simultaneously. He finished Alvarez in the second round to claim the lightweight title.

UFC 249

Ferguson vs Gaethje

At UFC 249 in 2020, Tony Ferguson faced Justin Gaethje for the interim lightweight title. Gaethje put on a striking clinic, dominating Ferguson and finishing him in the fifth round to claim the belt. It was a thrilling fight that showcased Gaethje's skills as a top contender.

UFC 261

Usman vs Masvidal 2

At UFC 261 in 2021, Kamaru Usman faced Jorge Masvidal for the welterweight title. Usman dominated the fight, knocking Masvidal out in the second round to retain his belt. It was a statement win that solidified Usman as one of the best welterweights in the sport.

UFC 229

Nurmagomedov vs McGregor

At UFC 229 in 2018, Khabib Nurmagomedov faced Conor McGregor for the lightweight title. Nurmagomedov dominated the fight, submitting McGregor in the fourth round to retain his belt. The fight was marred by a post-fight brawl, but it showcased Nurmagomedov's skills as a dominant champion.
